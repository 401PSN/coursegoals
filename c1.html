Collaborate as an effective team member and leader

Scroll Down

My interpetation

Building successful teams and businesses by working together is for me an art. Therefore C1 is more than just getting along with others; it's about creating an environment that encourages cooperation and benefits everyone. It requires the ability to listen, understand and communicate effectively with team members, as well as the willingness and ability to work with people different from you.

There are many ways to become effective at building effective teams. Some methods and techniques I've learned through my long career as a professional athlete in a team sport. Others I’ve recently learned here at digital media creative during the last years. And most certainly, some I will learn in the future. Some are more mindsets, but that way of thinking can be achieved more easily through specific formats.

For this last project, we were able to divide roles so that everybody in the team had moments throughout the project where they were leaders. That made everybody contribute as both a team member and team leader through different phases of the process. For me, I was the strategy lead. After letting our project manager kick us off, I naturally took the lead in the mid part of the project.

Leadership is fluid.

The leadership role I tried to take was a purpose-led leadership style or a ‘Leader from Within’. Some days it is meant to provide a vision and inspire others to take action to move toward that vision. An example of that visionary leadership was when dividing up the research into three categories, the brand, the business, and the user.

Later, during long days of research, I intended to provide more servant leadership, standing behind the team - coaching, motivating and empowering, calling forth brilliance in other people when they were doing great, but tough interviews during the discovery phase.

Leadership is about understanding what the group needs and having a repertoire of tools to support them.

- Isabel Shestopal, industry leader for this last module

Going into ideation, a collaborative environment needed to take place, so I tried to provide the team with involving leadership, standing beside, and creating connections between the members of the team. This wasto co-create the ideas we came up with, making it a team effort that everybody stands behind.

And lastly, when we were in doubt and didn’t move forward, I tried to provide courage and commitment to act, in a more intuitive leadership. During meetings when we got asked tough questions from the client, there were moments where the gut feeling was needed. Or just the courage to tell them that we don’t have the answers yet, but we will get there.

Reflection

Before the module, I read this course goal and did not understand how to capture “the leader” since I was not the project manager in this module.

Looking back, I realized that taking responsibility in a team is, of course, leadership. We all had different responsibilities in tasks and parts of the project. Kajsalisa was the PM. But I never saw her as our sole leader. Ellen took the lead in the production phase and Fredrik in the post-production. Together we all were both leaders and all team members at the same time. I had the last say in the decisions when it came to strategy, but it was never that black and white. I more set up the structure and guided the team to make decisions The same way for Ellen when we were in production. This is a clear differentiation of the way I think about a leader from before starting hyper island, and now.

I read Susan Wheeland's book creating effective teams, just before starting at Hyper Island. A part that resonated with me. She talked about how she met many members of groups being convinced that they can't make a difference. Statements like “unless the leader is replaced, there is nothing the rest of us can do”.

I’ve heard myself saying this many times as a hockey player. Now, years later, with more competence, I hesitate to blame others for group problems.
