C2

Work methodically and flexibly in internal team processes

Scroll Down

My interpetation

The link between team process and effective outcomes are well understood. However, it is also clear to me that processes can be complex, requiring the adaptation of a variety of approaches to decision-making, work design, and communication. For me, C2 is about creating, working in, and adapting to the needs of any specific team.

Starting at Hyper Island last year, I learned the format of Team Canva and Point of Departure. These two formats have been the starting point for all projects I’ve had. The purpose of these tools has been to define the structure, direction, and first steps of a project.

This part of the project is about preparation. For me, the best way to be agile and adapt is to be prepared. In the last project, we did a risk analysis to try to foresee different situations that could occur. This is a way to prepare, to be flexible.

Another example of methodically and flexibly in internal team processes was during the decision of roles in the team.

Due to a very open brief, we realized that we could not fully understand what roles were needed before we fully understood our mission.

Thanks to the team's experience, and to the structure the Team Canva gave us, we decided that we first needed to debrief. We handled it by only assigning a Project Manager, that together with the team divided tasks necessary to do the debriefing process. After the debriefing, which took almost two weeks, we could then decide on roles that were both necessary for team success, as well as meet all the team members' personal goals, needs, and expectations.

Reflection:

A team is like a factory. It has a set process for producing goods and services. The better the factory, the more efficient it is in producing.

I now have the skill to be a valuable member of creating efficient processes.

Sometimes things break in a factory, and someone needs to fix the problem. If the workers in the factory have the tools to solve the issues themselves, the faster it can go back to its full capacity. Workers with experience can both foresee errors, and deal with them faster than people that are new to them.

I now have experience seeing things break down, and I have successfully been able to foresee, repair, and improve these errors.

If the structure of the factory is too regulated, never leaving room for creativity from the employees, there will be no innovation. The structure should work to empower, not to control.

I now have the mindset to create balance between order and chaos is the best way to create an innovative climate.

In my last team, I was a part of putting together all these three parts. And with both personal and team goals being met, keeping low stress levels throughout the project, and receiving good feedback from the client - this proves to me I’m competent when it comes to working methodically and flexibly in internal team processes.
