S7

Work creatively to develop relevant concepts

Scroll Down

My interpetation

A message or idea is worthless unless you can deliver it. I interpret this course goal as the skill to create and design to enhance understanding. Whether the need is to communicate complex ideas internally, tell a story about your brand, or make a stakeholder understand the benefits of what you offer.

Engaging with the course goal

PolarCool AB, a leading company in concussion treatment for athletes, contacted us with a request to rebrand their organization. During a sprint format of 5 days, we worked on their brand identity, creating a design concept that enhanced and expressed their market-leading approach and determination to protect sports.

Working together in the creative process

As a start, we needed to understand to be relevant. Therefore, we interviewed four doctors and two experts in the area of ice hockey and concussions. We involved them in the creative process helping us get valuable feedback on new and previous material. We conclude how doctors want to receive information differs from the way players or Sport Managers want to receive information. This helped us in the creative process creating two different modes, one for each target group. Making the concept relevant for both occasions.

The team managed to capture the feeling and ideas we wanted to realize with this project. Quality, innovation, and the feeling for the small details are what stood out most for us at PolarCool throughout our collaboration. Can happily recommend this team if you, like us, want new innovative ideas in terms of branding and marketing materials for your company.”

- Erik Andersson, CEO of PolarCool

Throughout the creative process of developing the new concept, we aligned with each other. The mood board needed to fit the new mission statement. The new colors palette needed to work with both of the two modes envisioned by our strategy team. We did so by always feedbacking each other's progress, and always aligning with each other and the client.

One important step for the creative process was the style scapes. It helped align the team internally and with the client towards the same creative direction. As one of my teammates in charge of copywriting put it - writing slogans and brand wordings was much easier after seeing the design team's style guides.

As a final touch, together with the deliverables of a brand book and a new pitch deck presentation, we also package the concept in a visually pleasing way. This helped us show other ways of using our deliverables and also created more opportunities for our team and helped us land the job of rebuilding Polarcool’s web page based on our developed branding concept.

Reflection

Creating innovative solutions close to brief is not easy.

For me, the process is enjoyable but very draining when done well. Draining because for me to have an innovative climate, I need to surround myself with people that think differently from me. It also needs to be a climate where there is trust. A ‘yes and-’ mentality has time by time been proven to be effective both when it comes to sparking creativity within me, but also for me to spark creativity in others. You always need to be aware of the room, and very open to new impressions. This is for me very fun, but at the same time very draining.

I believe that a lot of great innovation and creative work lies where people's minds meet. In the mentioned workshop with Polarcool, we helped our client Erik, the CEO, to open up and co-create together with us. In a short sprint format, we would never be able to gain the knowledge of the company that Erik had. By merging our design knowledge with his understanding of the brand, business, and target group, we were able to merge these to and co-create a concept that in the end creates a sales success months later.

We hadn't been able to do it without Erik, and I believe that Erik took us in because he believed he could do it himself. Here lies also my biggest insight looking back on this module, and from this Polarcool sprint. With the effort you put into co-creating, you’ll get back a better result everybody will stand behind. Or as one of our lectures nicely put it: People Support What They Help Create.
