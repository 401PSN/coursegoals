S9

Design digital solutions considering user flow and aesthetics

My interpetation

In today's landscape, a great user experience is crucial when building something digital. My interpretation of this course goal is that S9 is about this skill. To have the skill and experience from mapping out a sequence of events that occur when a user interacts with a product or service, and build an easy to use and visually pleasing solution from it.

The user flow is a series of steps, or actions, that a user takes to complete a goal. It’s been a tool I've learned, used, and developed here at Hyper Island. I’ve now done it in several projects, both alone and as a part of a team.

The purpose has been to ensure to give the user the best experiences (user journey) interacting with the digital solution created - while staying within the boundaries of the client’s expectations. A well-designed user experience also ensures that the user can easily navigate through the application.

Three times my designs have been forwarded to developers, and I will give a quick run-through of one of them and how they interacted with the aesthetics of the design.

Gift Shop for Cervera

For Cervera I was freelancing together with two friends, creating a gift shop for the upcoming Christmas holidays. We started with an open brief, mentioning some necessary features and a few directions for the aesthetics like “should give a luxurious feeling” and “consider Cerveras visuals guidelines”.

Our first step, before creating our UI, was to design a user flow. The purpose was to create a blueprint of what would happen in every step of the journey of the user, and where the features mentioned in the brief should fit. It helped us map out all the parts and gave us a better overview of what needed to be done.

The user flow was followed throughout the project, with various uses like the explanation of functions, guiding the design, and presenting.

The flow contained and specified all the features of each screen, making it easy to understand and easy to code for the developers later.

The user flow also took into consideration things such as “investment” and “rewards”. It helped when creating the UI so that the parts where the investment from the user took place, was designed as smooth as possible, and the rewarding parts were more towards “a luxurious feeling” of the spectra.

The prototype we got was so, so nice. Far beyond expectations!

Nic Staeger E-commerce Manager at Cervera

Reflection

I remember playing the whispering game in school. The first kid told a story, and the story continued until it came back to the original storyteller - usually completely changed.

When making designs and sending them over to clients, developers, project managers, I think of this game. After just doing a few projects like this - I realized that the best way of avoiding this is to create a clear User Flow and a prototype with rules of the design (aesthetics) built on top of it.

The solution was based on existing graphic expression and system architecture and presented a clickable prototype that showed the entire customer journey and offers for the end consumer.

Henrik Örnstedt Business Area Manager at Cervera

As a designer that loves pleasing visuals, I tend to focus a lot on creating aesthetically pleasing frames and features. But sometimes it is not the ‘colorful experience brush’ to use, but the one that takes you from a-to-b in the smoothest possible way. I now have hands-on experience that a clear user flow in combination with my love for visuals, makes it more likely for me to pick the correct brush from the set.
