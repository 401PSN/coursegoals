S4

Assessing clients, and client contexts in order to determine needs and make informed recommendations

Scroll Down

My interpetation

Have the skill to collect data about clients, their surroundings, and any other part who have a stake in the matter. Then analyze it properly, and present the data as recommendations to create improvements.

Assessing

During this module, we got the challenge from adidas to help ​​kick off a new strategy cycle, with a completely new approach.

As the team’s Brand Strategist, I was in charge of understanding the client and stakeholders, engaging a lot with this course goal doing so. To solve the brief, my team and I needed to collect all the necessary information about our client and client context for us to both fully understand our mission, be able to solve it, and then package it in a useful way. We divided the research into three phases: Understand the business, understand the brand and understand the user.

Altho my biggest learnings regarding this course goal came from trying to understand the business and the brand; we used, tested, and improved our findings throughout the whole process.

My insights & approach as the lead strategist.

After collecting data during debriefing, stakeholder interviews, culture workshops, and risk analysis, we understood that no matter what our solution would be, there was some list of demands our solution to the brief needs to have to work. Also, it worked as recommendations for the strategy team to support their employees to embrace the work they are doing. In case our solution was not able to work out, this was a framework with identified needs and improvement areas to work with.

Reflection

I was again struck by how effective approach it is to initially put focus on assessing the client, the stakeholders, and the business perspective.

This brief enabled us to map out a framework that held our hand throughout the whole process. In the ideation, it was useful to evaluate solutions, when it helped ‘kill the darlings’ because the insights were not personal as the ideas were. It was used as a recommendation to the client in the end, which was a part of solving the brief.

A new insight I got from this brief, having this approach, was how it helped to present our final delivery. When first getting the brief, we thought we were helping the employees in the business units. But thanks to this approach, fully understanding the client and client context, we realized that for us to make the most impact, we needed to present our recommendations to the C-suite. This generated an approach from our side to instead focus our message, emphasizing how our solution was contributing to the (long term) vision of the company. This, in a wish, to make our recommendation come across.

Looking back on this project, integrating S4 as an important part of the design process, helped me avoid bias and wrongly preconceptions. It avoided some pitfalls like getting to trying to solve the problem, without understanding what to solve.

I will bring with me the skill and value of clients, and client contexts, so I can make informed recommendations close to brief, not only qualified guesses.
