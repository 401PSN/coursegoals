Legal issues and their impact on the digital industry

Scroll Down

My interpetation

In a fast-evolving digital environment, legal issues are constantly being discovered and amended. Online commerce has become more accessible, and legal concerns have increased. I've needed to gain relevant knowledge to tackle these issues in the digital field.

Learning and engaging with the course goal

During a lecture by Malki Afram, Lawyer and partner at Startup Juristerna, we learned about the rights of consumers and the importance of transparency. Sweden has some of the strongest laws on data and privacy, as it is a part of EU law and the GDPR. In an interesting discussion, we talked about how the issue was more about enforcing the existing legislation, preventing Sweden from becoming a ‘safe haven’ for bad actors.

Personal learnings and reflections

For me aiming to work within digital marketing, some topics are more relevant than others. One very relevant is cookies. Recent developments have meant that those relying on cookies to provide relevant advertising are being penalized, and this could result in a decline in the effectiveness of advertising in general.

One need not exclude the other, and it is high time for a better balance between protection and progress.

- Carolina Brånby - Director Digital policy at Confederation of Swedish Enterprise

I believe that both cookies and regulations are put in place of good intention. The cookies are there to create a tailor-made experience and create value. My feeds are much better now than before, providing me content more relevant to me. The regulations are there to protect from misuse, overuse, and privacy is also a keystone in a functional democracy.

To find the balance is not only a lawmakers responsibility, but also actors. For me, as an actor, I always have to balance the use of data so that it brings value and not harm (immediate, or future) for my user. As a positive effect of this, it will build trust in the people I interact with, and create a win-win situation.

Another relevant topic in this field is copyright and it affects both me as an individual and the companies I work with. In the case of me as an individual, infringement can incur fines, and in the case of companies, heavy fines. For me in the digital industry, it's the EUCD (European Copyright Directive), equal to the American DMCA, that is the most relevant copywriting law.

For instance, in this font you are reading, I need to make sure I have the correct license, to use it. Otherwise, I will put myself, or the people I work for at risk. Being able to make sure I'm allowed to use the picture in the header, an asset from the site Envato elements has become a natural part of my work as a digital designer, thanks to the learnings I gained during this module.

Recap

I now have a basic understanding, together with knowledge about the importance of updating what I know about legal impacts regularly. I’m not going to know all of the different paragraphs regarding the digital industry today. And if I do, it will be old news tomorrow.
