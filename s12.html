S12

Analyse an organisation’s culture and procedures
to give relevant recommendations for change

Scroll Down

My interpetation

Organizations change all the time. People are hired, projects are initiated, things go wrong. The way an organization handles these changes depends on its' culture and procedures. For me, S12 is about the skill to analyze key values, beliefs, and attitudes that shape that culture. It will enable me to identify gaps in existing practice or provide new ideas for improvement.

From day one

Reading from the Hyper Island webpage, the school helps create dynamic and innovative cultures that accelerate long-term change. Therefore I believe I've interacted with this course goal from the first day starting this program last year.

But more focus than ever was put on this during this last module. Getting the adidas brief mentioned in S4, we understood that it was a very complex one. An important part of creating a successfull outcome, was to allign the culture with this new strategy, in order to allow it to be embraced.

Culture eats strategy for breakfast

- Klara Adolphson | CEO & Founder Innovation Leadership Group

Trying to make a cultural change in a billion-dollar company during a short period is of course hard. Thankfully, we had guidance from several brilliant lectures and workshops in our task. As a first step to narrow down the scope, we tried to pinpoint what we could bring to the table that they needed.

With this approach, we were able to focus our research around what challenges and opportunities Adidas has in their culture in their urge to include and involve a gen z female perspective.

Working in parallel, splitting up the team's research, we could collect independent information about both gen z and adidas's internal culture. This allowed us to compare the two afterward. We looked for similarities, differences, and opportunities.

Similarities that we later could recommend to adidas to enhance. Differences that we could recommend adidas to bridge the gap between. All this we could then present as new opportunities for the strategy team to work with so that the culture would not kill the strategy, but instead enhance this new approach within the company.

Reflection

To be able to draw a straight line between the fact that adidas is one of the most loved brands of the generation, and the similarities in culture between adidas and generation z was an aha-moment.

I truly believed that culture eats strategy for breakfast. In this project, I finally got to see some hard facts which backed up my beliefs.

Of course, it seems obvious in retrospect. But my initial thought was that the reason was that gen z resonated with adidas because of the pleasing content, marketing efforts, and products. And of course, it is still true. But after analyzing the culture, it's clear to me that the foundation for the successful marketing efforts and products are people working at adidas; sharing values, vision, and mission with gen z.

The work that kept recurring when researching gen z was “authenticity”.
My biggest insight from this module is that you can fake a strategy, but you can't fake a culture.
